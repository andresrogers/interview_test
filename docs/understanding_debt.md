# Understanding debt

This page describes something we look for across **every** challenge in this
repository, so we've written it once here rather than repeating it in each one.

These challenges can all be driven with an agent — that's the point. We want to
see you use agentic workflows to produce far more, and faster, than you could
alone. But there's one way that goes wrong, and it's the thing we're really
evaluating for: **understanding debt.**

Understanding debt is what you accrue when you let an agent do the *understanding*
for you, not just the work: the output gets produced, but the comprehension that
should come with it never transfers to you. Like any debt it's invisible at first
— the result looks finished, even impressive — and it comes due later, usually the
moment something is wrong or a real decision has to be made. That's why it's
dangerous: judgment in a domain comes from understanding that domain, so when you
delegate the understanding you delegate the judgment with it. You're left unable to
tell whether the agent's output is right, or to steer it toward a better direction.
The leverage is real — but it can only endure if you remain the one who understands.

So across these challenges the bar is the same: produce great work fast, with
whatever tools you like, and still be able to explain, defend, and steer it as if
you'd reasoned it out yourself. We'd take a result you can stand behind over an
impressive one you can't — every time.

## Further reading

The same phenomenon shows up in the literature under two names:

- [Comprehension debt — Addy Osmani](https://addyosmani.com/blog/comprehension-debt/)
  ([O'Reilly Radar version](https://www.oreilly.com/radar/comprehension-debt-the-hidden-cost-of-ai-generated-code/)):
  "the growing gap between how much code exists in your system and how much of it
  any human being genuinely understands" — it breeds false confidence and comes due
  at the worst moment.
- [Your Brain on ChatGPT — MIT Media Lab](https://www.media.mit.edu/publications/your-brain-on-chatgpt/)
  ([arXiv 2506.08872](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.08872)): coins *cognitive debt* —
  outsourcing thinking to an AI defers effort now at the cost of measurably weaker
  recall and comprehension of your own output.

Our framing aims one level up from the code: understanding the **problem and the
domain** — the layer that produces judgment, not just a result that compiles.
