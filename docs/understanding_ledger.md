# The understanding ledger

Submit a short **understanding ledger** alongside your work on each challenge (or
one for the whole submission — your call). It is an honest accounting of *where
your understanding is, and isn't.*

For the work you're handing in, briefly record:

- **What you verified yourself** — the parts of the result you derived, checked,
  or could reconstruct from first principles, and *how* you checked them.
- **What you delegated and trusted** — the parts you let an agent produce and did
  not fully verify, and *why* trusting the agent there was a reasonable call.
- **What you're unsure of** — the assumptions, edge cases, and limits you're aware
  of, and where the answer is most likely to be wrong.

**Why we ask.** See [what we value](./understanding_debt.md). The ledger is *not*
a confession — delegating the right things to an agent is the skill we're hiring
for. What we're looking for is an accurate map of your own understanding, because
having an accurate map is itself evidence that you have the understanding. A
candidate in understanding debt can't write an honest ledger.

**Keep it short** — a page is plenty across the whole submission. And expect us to
**probe it**: in review we may ask you to explain, from first principles,
something you listed under "verified." That's the whole game.
